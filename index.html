These bats love to eat aquatic insects like mosquitos, mayflies, beetles, and moths, and can eat about 1,200 insects in an hour. They are very common throughout North America ranging from Alaska to Southern California. They are vital to controlling insect populations. They are most commonly associated with humans, as beside roosting in trees, they form nursery colonies of the hundred and thousands in buildings, attics, and manmade structures.

Little Brown Bat

These bats love eating moths! They also eat beetles, flies, crickets, and true bugs. They use echolocation. They are common throughout North America and Central America, they roost in trees. Seasonal migration to warmer climates as well as hibernation is part of their behavior.
Who am I?
Hoary Bat

This bat is the only bat in the United States to have large ears and a nose leaf. It is also one of the most maneuverable in flight. With short and broad wings, it can fly at low speeds using minimal energy. It has superior eyesight that allows it to capture insects direcly from foliage or the ground rather than in flight. This bat prefers to eat beetles, crickets, grasshoppers, and sphinx moths. Human disturbance and habitat loss is major issue for this bat.
